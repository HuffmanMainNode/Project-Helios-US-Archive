# Federalist No 51

**Description:** An essay by James Madison addressing means by which appropriate checks and balances can be created in government.
**Source Citation:** [Library of Congress](https://guides.loc.gov/federalist-papers/text-51-60#s-lg-box-wrapper-25493323)

## Text
To what expedient, then, shall we finally resort, for maintaining in practice the necessary partition of power among the several departments, as laid down in the Constitution? The only answer that can be given is, that as all these exterior provisions are found to be inadequate, the defect must be supplied, by so contriving the interior structure of the government as that its several constituent parts may, by their mutual relations, be the means of keeping each other in their proper places...
