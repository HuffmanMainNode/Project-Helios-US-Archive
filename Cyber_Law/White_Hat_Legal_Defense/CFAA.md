# Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)

## Legal Summary
The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA), codified at 18 U.S.C. § 1030, is the primary federal statute used to prosecute hacking and unauthorized access to computers. Enacted in 1984, it has been amended multiple times to expand its scope to virtually any computer connected to the internet. The law protects 'protected computers,' which include government systems, financial institution systems, and any computer used in interstate or foreign commerce.

## Full Statutory Language
18 U.S.C. § 1030 - Fraud and related activity in connection with computers:
(a) Whoever—
(1) having knowingly accessed a computer without authorization or exceeding authorized access, and by means of such conduct having obtained information that has been determined by the United States Government pursuant to an Executive order or statute to require protection against unauthorized disclosure for reasons of national defense or foreign relations... shall be punished as provided in subsection (c) of this section.
(2) intentionally accesses a computer without authorization or exceeds authorized access, and thereby obtains—
(A) information contained in a financial record of a financial institution, or of a card issuer as defined in section 1602(n) of title 15, or in a file of a consumer reporting agency on a consumer...
