# Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA)

## Legal Summary
The Electronic Communications Privacy Act of 1986 (ECPA) was passed to extend government restrictions on wire taps from telephone calls to include transmissions of electronic data by computer. It consists of three main parts: the Wiretap Act, the Stored Communications Act (SCA), and the Pen Register Act. It establishes the legal framework for how law enforcement can access stored and in-transit electronic communications.

## Full Statutory Language
The Electronic Communications Privacy Act of 1986 (Pub. L. 99-508, Oct. 21, 1986, 100 Stat. 1848):
Title I of the ECPA protects wire, oral, and electronic communications while in transit. It sets forth the procedures the government must follow to intercept such communications.
Title II of the ECPA (the Stored Communications Act) addresses the privacy of stored wire and electronic communications and transactional records held by third-party internet service providers (ISPs).
